Socioeconomic Implications of Single Parent Families

Researchers have identified the rise in single-parent families (especially mother-child families) as a major factor driving the long-term increase in child poverty in the United States.
Growing up in single-parent households has been shown to increase the risk of children dropping out of school, disconnecting from the labor force, and becoming teen parents.
Children in lower-income, single-parent families face the most significant barriers to success in school and the work force.
